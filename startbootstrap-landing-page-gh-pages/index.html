Circuit Validation/Design

 

Circuit Validation/Design

Validation is often neglected in the grand scheme of things as it isn't directly seen most of the time in the eyes of the consumer. However, this is thinking could not be more wrong. Good validation ensures that a product release is free of bugs and works exactly as expected when released to the public.


Circuit design and validation go hand and hand. Good circuit design combined with good validation helps ensure a solid product and reduces the chance of angry customers when the product is released.

Quality Assurance

 

Quality Assurance

Quality Assurance is a key portion in the development of any product. Without having QA as part of the general process for creating a product, defects or malfunctions could be widespread. A product once planned to solve problems might end up creating even more of them! Quality assurance makes sure that product will meet and possibly even exceed customer demands, while also minimizing defects as much as possible.


Whether it is code inspection, regression testing, or running test cases, thoroughly checking for potential bugs and solving them is essential to a good QA process.

Portfolio

Senior Capstone Design

 

Senior Capstone Design

For my senior capstone design project in Oregon State University, I was tasked to design a system that would illuminate a firefighting drafting hose so the hose could be better seen in low visibility conditions. One of the major problems with water drafting is that air bubbles during the drafting process (loss of prime) will cause firefighters to lose valuable time that can be used to put out a fire. To resolve this, a small buzzer alarm was sounded as soon as a loss of prime was detected inside the hose.


We worked closely with the lead firefighter in a firefighting district to make a good prototype product that they could later expand on to potentially put onto market in the future. I worked in a group of 3 over the course of 9 months to accomplish this.


This was my first "real" exposure to working in a high stakes group setting. We had to have weekly group updates to notify each other as well as the project manager what was accomplished during the week and what was still left to be done. This project taught me good communication skills and organization are essential to being a good engineer.


Check out the project website!

I am familiar with:

  • Collaborating in a group setting
  • Working under pressure
  • kiCAD for PCB designing
